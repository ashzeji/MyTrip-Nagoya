Places I Visited

  • Nagoya Castle

    Nagoya Castle was the first major stop of the trip. The golden shachihoko ornaments on the roof are one of the most recognizable symbols of the city, and the surrounding grounds were a nice place to walk around before heading inside.

    The castle museum inside had some interesting exhibits on the history of the building and the surrounding area.

  • Osu Shopping District

    Osu is a busy, covered shopping street with a mix of old and new shops, temples tucked between buildings, and plenty of places to grab a quick snack.

    It felt very different from the more formal atmosphere at the castle, in a good way.

  • Atsuta Shrine

    Atsuta Shrine is one of the most important Shinto shrines in Japan. The grounds are surrounded by tall trees, which made it feel much quieter and calmer than the rest of the city.

    It was a good last stop to end the day on a relaxed note.
